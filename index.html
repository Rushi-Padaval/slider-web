LION
The lion (Panthera leo) is a large carnivorous mammal, native to Africa and parts of Asia. Known for their distinctive mane (in males) and social behavior, lions live in groups called prides. As apex predators, they primarily hunt large herbivores. Lions are symbols of strength and are featured prominently in various cultures and wildlife conservation efforts.
WOLF
The wolf (Canis lupus) is a highly adaptable carnivorous mammal found in diverse habitats worldwide. Known for its social structure within packs, wolves communicate through vocalizations and body language. They play crucial roles in ecosystems by regulating prey populations. Despite conservation efforts, some wolf populations face threats, including habitat loss and human-wolf conflicts.
ELEPHANT
The elephant, belonging to the family Elephantidae, is a large herbivorous mammal found in Africa and Asia. Recognized for their distinct trunk, tusks, and massive size, elephants display high intelligence and complex social structures. Threatened by habitat loss and poaching, conservation efforts aim to protect these majestic creatures and their vital role in ecosystems.
TIGER
The tiger (Panthera tigris) is a powerful carnivorous mammal native to Asia. Known for its distinctive orange coat with black stripes, tigers are solitary hunters and excellent swimmers. Endangered due to habitat loss and poaching for their body parts, conservation efforts focus on protecting their habitats and combating illegal wildlife trade.
